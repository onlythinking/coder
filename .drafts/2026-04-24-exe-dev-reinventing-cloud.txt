Title: A Go core contributor argues today's cloud abstractions are "the wrong shape"

Body: David Crawshaw (Go core contributor, startup co-founder) just published his reasoning for building a new cloud platform called exe.dev. His core arguments:
- VMs are tied to CPU/memory, limiting elasticity
- Remote storage overhead went from 10% (HDD era) to 10x (SSD era)
- Cloud egress pricing is 10x what you pay in a normal data center

He wants to expose real physical computers through the cloud API. Interesting read for anyone dealing with cloud vendor lock-in.
